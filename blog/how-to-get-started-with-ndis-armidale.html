How to Get Started with the NDIS in Armidale

If you or someone you care about has a disability and lives in Armidale or the New England Region, the NDIS (National Disability Insurance Scheme) could fund the support you need. But navigating the NDIS for the first time can feel overwhelming. Here's a straightforward guide to getting started.

Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

To access the NDIS, you generally need to meet these criteria: you're aged between 7 and 65, you're an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and you have a permanent and significant disability that affects your ability to take part in everyday activities. Your disability can be physical, intellectual, cognitive, neurological, visual, hearing, or psychosocial.

If you're not sure whether you're eligible, your GP in Armidale can help you understand your situation and provide supporting documentation.

Step 2: Make an Access Request

You can apply to the NDIS by calling 1800 800 110 or visiting your nearest NDIS office. In the New England Region, you can also speak with a Local Area Coordinator (LAC) who can help you through the process. You'll need to provide evidence of your disability, which can come from your doctor, specialist, or allied health professional in Armidale.

Step 3: Planning Meeting

Once approved, you'll have a planning meeting where you discuss your goals, daily challenges, and what support you need. Think about what you want to achieve: Do you want to live more independently? Build social connections? Learn new skills? Get help with daily tasks? These goals will shape your NDIS plan and the funding you receive.

Step 4: Understanding Your Plan

Your NDIS plan will outline your funded supports, which typically fall into three categories:

  • Core Supports: Everyday assistance like personal care, transport, household tasks, and community access
  • Capacity Building: Support to develop skills, find employment, and build independence
  • Capital Supports: Funding for equipment, home modifications, or specialist disability accommodation

You can choose to self-manage your plan, use a plan manager, or have the NDIA manage it for you.

Step 5: Choose a Provider in Armidale

This is the most important step. Look for a provider who is locally based in Armidale, has experienced support workers, offers the specific services in your plan, and takes a person-centred approach. At Asina Disability Support, we're based right here at 14 Shambrook Ave, Armidale, and we offer all core NDIS services across the New England Region.

Step 6: Service Agreement and Getting Started

Once you choose a provider, you'll sign a service agreement that outlines what services you'll receive, how often, and how they'll be delivered. At Asina, we work with you (and your support coordinator if you have one) to make this process as simple as possible. Most participants start receiving support within a few days of signing their agreement.

Tips for New NDIS Participants in Armidale

Keep a record of your goals and how your supports are helping you achieve them — this is valuable at plan review time. Don't be afraid to speak up if something isn't working. Connect with local disability advocacy groups in Armidale for additional support. And remember, your NDIS plan can be reviewed and adjusted as your needs change.

Need Help Getting Started?

If you're new to the NDIS and want guidance, Asina Disability Support is happy to help. Call us on 1300 851 190 or submit a referral online. We'll walk you through the process and help you access the support you deserve in Armidale and the New England Region.
