## What Is Supported Independent Living?

Supported Independent Living, or SIL, is one of the most important NDIS supports available for people who need help with daily tasks in their own home. It's designed for participants who want to live as independently as possible while still having access to the support they need.

In Armidale and the New England Region, SIL can look different depending on the person. Some participants live in shared homes with other NDIS participants, while others live alone with regular support visits. The common thread is that SIL is about building skills, growing confidence, and creating a living situation that works for you.

## What Does SIL Include?

SIL support in Armidale typically covers:

- **Personal care** — Showering, dressing, grooming, and hygiene support.
- **Meal support** — Help with meal planning, cooking, and kitchen skills.
- **Medication management** — Reminders and assistance with medications.
- **Household tasks** — Cleaning, laundry, and keeping the home tidy.
- **Life skills coaching** — Budgeting, time management, and building routines.
- **Overnight support** — Active or sleepover support depending on your needs.
- **Community participation** — Getting out of the house and connecting with the Armidale community.

## How Is SIL Funded?

SIL is funded through your NDIS plan under the Core Supports category. The amount of funding you receive depends on your individual needs and the level of support required. A support coordinator can help you understand how SIL fits into your plan and what options are available in Armidale.
