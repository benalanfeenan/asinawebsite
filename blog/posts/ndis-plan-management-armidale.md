## How Is Your NDIS Plan Managed?

When you receive your NDIS plan, one of the first decisions you'll make is how it's managed. This determines who handles the paperwork, pays the invoices, and keeps track of your budget.

There are three options, and each has its own advantages depending on your situation.

## NDIA Managed

With NDIA management, the National Disability Insurance Agency handles your funding directly. Your providers submit invoices to the NDIA, and the NDIA pays them from your plan.

This is the most hands-off option. You don't need to worry about paperwork or tracking spending. The trade-off is that you can only use registered NDIS providers, which can limit your choices in smaller towns like Armidale.

## Plan Managed

Plan management is the middle ground. A plan manager handles your invoices and budget tracking on your behalf. The big advantage is that you can use both registered and unregistered providers, giving you more flexibility in who supports you.

Many participants in Armidale prefer plan management because it opens up more options without the paperwork burden of self-management. Plan managers are funded separately through your NDIS plan, so it doesn't reduce your support budget.

## Self Managed

Self-management gives you full control. You pay providers directly, claim reimbursements from the NDIA, and manage your own budget. You can use any provider, set your own rates (within NDIS guidelines), and have complete flexibility.

It's the most work, but for participants who want total control over their supports, it can be the best option. You'll need to keep records, manage invoices, and stay on top of your spending.

## Which Option Is Best?

There's no single right answer. It depends on how involved you want to be and what level of flexibility you need.

If you're unsure, start with plan management. It gives you flexibility without the admin overhead, and you can always switch to self-management later as you get more comfortable.
